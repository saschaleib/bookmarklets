Unicode Math Pseudofont Substitutions

These bookmarklets also replace plain characters with Unicode alternatives. The difference with the standard character replacement scripts is that these here use characters from the SMP, which aren't fully supported by all platforms (notably iOS and Android fail to display these characters).

This means you should avoid using these (at the time of this writing at least) for text that might be displayed on common mobile platforms (e.g. on social media).

On common desktop platforms, they seem to work fine (at least under Windows 7, 10 and MacOS 10.11, where I have tested it).

You should also keep in mind that these characters were designed to have a different semantic meaning than the regular characters: they are symbols for mathematical typesetting, and most programs will not recognise words that are created entirely or even just partly from such characters as equivalent to words composed of regular characters.

Most notably, Google will not recognise these as words even!

You should also be aware that hyphenation or spell-checking may not work as expected with these characters.
